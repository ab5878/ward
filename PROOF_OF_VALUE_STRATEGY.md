# Ward v0 - Proof of Value Strategy
**The Problem**: Product works, but zero customers = zero proof of value

---

## The Catch-22

**To Get Customers**: Need proof Ward saves time/money  
**To Get Proof**: Need customers using Ward

**We Must Break This Cycle**

---

## Strategy 1: Live Disruption Demo (Tomorrow)

### The Play
During demo, ASK: **"Do you have a live disruption happening right now?"**

### If YES (50% chance):
1. **Document their current approach** (5 mins):
   - "Walk me through exactly what you're doing right now"
   - Count: Calls made, WhatsApp messages, people involved
   - Note: Who knows what, any confusion?

2. **Solve it together using Ward** (15 mins):
   - Driver reports via voice (or you simulate it)
   - Manager performs RCA
   - Assign ownership + actions
   - Track resolution

3. **Compare in real-time**:
   - Before Ward: "You made 7 calls, 3 WhatsApp groups, 2 people didn't know status"
   - With Ward: "All context in one place, RCA done in 3 mins, everyone notified"
   - Time saved: Visible immediately

### Why This Works:
✅ Real disruption = real proof  
✅ Side-by-side comparison = undeniable  
✅ They feel the difference = emotional buy-in  
✅ Immediate value = no "wait for pilot"

## Strategy 2: Shadow & Measure (Week 1 Pilot)

### The Play
**Offer**: "Let me shadow your team for 1 week to prove Ward's value"

### Week Plan:

**Days 1-2: BEFORE Ward (Baseline)**
- Shadow 5 disruptions handled their normal way
- Measure:
  1. Time from discovery to resolution
  2. Number of calls/messages
  3. People involved
  4. Context lost (new person joins, what do they know?)
  5. Decision clarity (did they know WHY it happened?)
- Document everything with timestamps

**Days 3-5: WITH Ward (Comparison)**
- Same team handles next 5 disruptions using Ward
- Measure same 5 metrics
- Track: Voice usage, RCA performed?, timeline completeness

**Day 5: Results Presentation**
- Side-by-side comparison table
- Time saved per disruption
- Context loss: Before (high) vs After (zero)
- Team feedback: "Was Ward easier or harder?"

### Example Results Table:

Context Loss Incidents  | 3           | 0          | 100% better
RCA Performed          | 0           | 5          | New capability
Team Satisfaction      | 5/10        | 8/10       | 60% better

### Why This Works:
✅ Hard data = undeniable proof  
✅ Before/after = clear comparison

## Strategy 3: Single Disruption Reconstruction

### The Play
**Ask**: "What was your worst disruption in the last 3 months?"

### Process (During Demo):
1. **Collect the story** (10 mins):
   - What happened?
   - How did you discover it?
   - Who was involved?
   - What actions were taken?
   - How long to resolve?
   - What went wrong?

2. **Reconstruct in Ward** (10 mins):
   - Create case with their description
   - Add timeline events as they tell the story
   - Perform RCA on their case
   - Show what recommendations Ward would have made

3. **Compare**:
   - What they did: "7 people, 3 days, customer escalated"
   - What Ward suggests: "RCA shows root cause was X, could have assigned to Y within 2 hours"
   - Gap analysis: "Why did this take 3 days?"

### Example Reconstruction:
**Their Story**:
> "Container stuck at JNPT for 5 days. We thought customs hold, but actually port congestion. Customer called CEO. Scrambled team. Finally cleared on day 5."

**Ward's RCA**:
> "Root Cause: Port congestion (not customs). JNPT avg wait was 3 days that week (monsoon delay).  
> Recommended Action: Check port status FIRST (5 min). If congestion, set customer expectation immediately.  
> Prevented: Customer escalation, CEO involvement, team scrambling.  
> Time Saved: 3 days of stress + customer relationship damage."

### Why This Works:
✅ They relive the pain = emotional connection  
✅ Ward shows better way = "wish we had this then"  
✅ Retrospective proof = "could have saved us"  
✅ No waiting for pilot = immediate insight
