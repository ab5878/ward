# Ward v0 - Market Opportunity Analysis (TAM/SAM/SOM)

**Thesis:** Ward is not just "Logistics Software"; it is an **Active Intelligence Layer** targeting the **$184 Billion** annual loss caused by supply chain disruptions. While incumbents focus on *tracking* (visibility), Ward focuses on *fixing* (resolution).

---

## 1. Market Sizing

### **TAM (Total Addressable Market)**
**Global Supply Chain Management Software**
*   **Value:** **$38.5 Billion** (2025 Estimate)
*   **Growth:** ~14.6% CAGR
*   **Definition:** All organizations globally purchasing software for TMS, WMS, and Visibility.
*   **Source:** MarketsandMarkets, Grand View Research.

### **SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)**
**Indian Logistics & "Unorganized" Coordination**
*   **Total Logistics Spend:** **$349 Billion** (India, 2025)
*   **The Inefficiency Gap:** India spends ~14% of GDP on logistics (vs. 8-10% in developed nations).
*   **Tech Spending (Est):** ~$5-7 Billion (Software + Services)
*   **The Target:** The **90% Unorganized Sector**. India's logistics is dominated by small fleet owners, independent CHAs, and manual brokers who cannot use complex ERPs like SAP TM.

### **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)**
**Disruption Resolution Platform (The "Ward" Niche)**
*   **Value:** **$1.2 Billion** (Initial Wedge)
*   **Calculation:** Capturing 1% of the "Cost of Disruption" savings in the Indian/Emerging market.
*   **Focus:** Mid-market Forwarders, Transporters, and Enterprise Shippers who need to bridge the gap between their ERP and the chaotic real world.

*   **Too Rigid:** SAP/Oracle require structured data. They break when a driver says "Tyre burst, waiting for mechanic."
*   **Language Barrier:** Global tools are English-first. They fail with a Hindi-speaking driver or Gujarati-speaking CHA.
*   **Passive:** They alert you *after* the delay. Ward actively coordinates the fix.

## 4. Ward's Strategic "Wedge"

How do we enter and dominate?

1.  **The "Voice" Wedge:**
    *   **Problem:** 90% of field data is lost because drivers won't type.
    *   **Solution:** Ward's "Voice-First" reporting (powered by Sarvam AI) captures ground truth that P44/FourKites miss. This creates a proprietary **Data Moat**.

2.  **The "Active Agent" Wedge:**
    *   **Problem:** Ops teams drown in coordination.
    *   **Solution:** Ward's "Active Coordination" agents automate the low-value follow-ups ("Where is the invoice?"), acting as a force multiplier.

3.  **The "Platform" Play:**
    *   **Strategy:** Don't replace the TMS. **Embed** into it.
    *   **Mechanism:** Webhooks, Magic Links, and APIs allow Ward to be the "Intelligence Engine" inside existing workflows (Cargowise, SAP, Custom ERPs).
